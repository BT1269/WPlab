Haji Ali Dargah

The Haji Ali Dargah is a renowned mosque and tomb located on a small islet off the coast of Worli, Mumbai.
Built in 1431, it commemorates the memory of Sayyed Pir Haji Ali Shah Bukhari, a wealthy merchant-turned-saint.

History

Legend has it that Haji Ali renounced all his wealth before embarking on a pilgrimage to Mecca.The dargah was constructed
at the spot where he was believed to have passed away. It is an exquisite example of Indo-Islamic architecture, featuring a 
marble structure with intricate detailing.

Significance

Haji Ali Dargah is not just a place of worship but also a symbol of communal harmony, attracting visitors of all faiths.
The serene location amidst the Arabian Sea makes it a spiritual and architectural marvel, drawing millions of devotees and 
tourists each year.
