Elephanta Caves

The Elephanta Caves are a network of sculpted caves located on Elephanta Island in Mumbai Harbour.
Dating back to the 5th to 8th centuries, these caves are primarily dedicated to Lord Shiva and showcase intricate 
rock-cut sculptures and carvings.

History

The caves were originally known as Gharapuri, meaning "the city of caves," and served as a place of worship for centuries.
The Portuguese named the island Elephanta after discovering a large stone elephant statue near the shore, which is now 
preserved in the Bhau Daji Lad Museum.

Significance

A UNESCO World Heritage Site, the Elephanta Caves are renowned for their artistic excellence.
The centerpiece is the iconic Trimurti sculpture, a 20-foot-high masterpiece depicting the three faces of Lord Shiva.
The caves attract history enthusiasts and pilgrims from around the world.
