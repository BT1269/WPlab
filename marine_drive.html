Marine Drive

Marine Drive is a 3.6-kilometer-long boulevard in South Mumbai, stretching along the Arabian Sea.Also known as 
the "Queen's Necklace," it is famous for its stunning views and crescent-shaped coastline that glows beautifully 
under the city lights at night.

History

Constructed in the 1920s and 1930s, Marine Drive was part of Mumbai's urban planning to connect 
the city with reclaimed land. It became a major recreational spot for residents and tourists, 
hosting events and parades, and symbolizing Mumbai's cosmopolitan spirit.

Significance

Marine Drive is a cultural and recreational hub, attracting joggers, couples, and families.
It is a prime example of Art Deco architecture, with several buildings lining the promenade 
now designated as a UNESCO World Heritage Site. The iconic sunset views make it one
of Mumbai's most beloved landmarks.
